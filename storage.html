// COMPONENT 04

Storage.
The Digital Vault.

Every installed game. Every photo. Your operating system. It all lives in your Storage — ready to be called up whenever you need it.

In plain English: Storage is your computer's filing cabinet. Unlike RAM, which forgets everything when the PC turns off, your storage drive keeps your files safe permanently. Faster storage means faster loading screens and a snappier PC.

// THE BIG CHOICE

NVMe vs SATA SSDs.

Hard drives (HDDs) are dead for modern gaming. Today, the choice is between the ultra-fast NVMe format and the older SATA SSD format. Here is what separates them.

NVMe M.2

// THE CURRENT STANDARD · PLUGS INTO MOTHERBOARD

The modern standard for all PCs. These tiny drives look like a stick of gum and plug directly into your motherboard. They are incredibly fast, eliminating cables and saving space.

Key specs

  • Speed range — 3000 – 14000 MB/s
  • Connection — PCIe lanes (Direct to CPU)
  • Cables needed — None
  • Form factor — M.2 2280

SATA SSD

// OLDER TECH · USES CABLES

The older standard. While still 5x faster than a mechanical hard drive, they are limited by the SATA cable interface. Good for adding cheap secondary storage, but not ideal for a primary boot drive today.

Key specs

  • Speed range — 500 – 550 MB/s
  • Connection — SATA Controller
  • Cables needed — Data Cable + Power Cable
  • Form factor — 2.5 Inch Block
// THE REAL QUESTION

How much capacity?

Modern games are massive, with some titles taking over 150GB alone. Buying too little storage now means deleting games constantly later.

💾

512 GB

The bare minimum

Enough for Windows, basic apps, and maybe 2-3 large modern games. You will run out of space very quickly. Only recommended for extreme budget office builds.

Use case: Budget office PC only

🎮

1 TB

The starting point

The absolute minimum for a modern gaming PC. Holds your OS, essential software, and a respectable library of games. A solid starting point for most users.

Use case: Entry gaming, daily use

🎬

2 TB+

The sweet spot

The ideal capacity in 2026. Plenty of room for a large game library, high-res video files, and system backups without constantly worrying about your storage bar turning red.

Use case: Heavy gaming, Content creation

// 2026 SNAPSHOT

Modern standards.

The specs you'll see on every Storage product page — and what they actually mean. Gens, form factors, protocols: here's your decoder.

Standard What it means Status (2026)
PCIe Gen 5 NVMe The cutting edge. Speeds up to 14,000 MB/s. Runs very hot and requires a large heatsink. Overkill for gaming. MODERN
PCIe Gen 4 NVMe The sweet spot. Speeds up to 7,500 MB/s. Perfect for PS5 and modern high-end PCs. MODERN
PCIe Gen 3 NVMe Older NVMe generation. Speeds up to 3,500 MB/s. Still incredibly fast and great for budget builds. AGING
SATA III SSD Maxes out around 550 MB/s. Uses cables. Good for secondary mass storage. AGING
HDD (7200 RPM) Mechanical drive. Slowest option (~150 MB/s). Only useful for archiving photos/videos/backups. ARCHIVE ONLY
M.2 2280 The physical size of most NVMe drives (22mm wide, 80mm long). MODERN
DirectStorage A new API that lets the GPU load game assets directly from an NVMe SSD, reducing load times. MODERN
// PICK YOUR DRIVE

Buying guide.

Don't overpay for speeds you won't notice. The difference between a Gen 3 and Gen 5 SSD in game loading screens is often less than 1 second.

🏷️

Budget Build

~ $50 – $70

A 1 TB PCIe Gen 3 NVMe. It's affordable, eliminates cables, and provides a snappy Windows experience. Don't bother with SATA SSDs unless your motherboard lacks M.2 slots.

Pick: 1 TB PCIe Gen 3 NVMe

⚡

Gaming Build

~ $120 – $150

A 2 TB PCIe Gen 4 NVMe. This is the sweet spot. It offers blistering speeds, takes advantage of DirectStorage for future games, and 2TB gives you breathing room for massive game libraries.

Pick: 2 TB PCIe Gen 4 NVMe

🖥️

Creator / Pro

~ $250 – $350

A 4 TB PCIe Gen 4 (or Gen 5 if doing heavy 8K video editing). Creators move massive files constantly, making the high sequential speeds of top-tier NVMe drives actually worth the premium.

Pick: 4 TB PCIe Gen 4 NVMe

// THE WIRING DIAGRAM

Compatibility & dependencies.

Storage connects your data to the rest of the system. The CPU provides the data lanes, the motherboard provides the physical slots, and the OS formats it so you can actually use it.

CPU Provides PCIe Lanes
MOTHERBOARD Hosts M.2/SATA slots
STORAGE You are here
PSU Powers SATA drives
OS Formats the drive

What each motherboard supports

Match your motherboard generation to the correct NVMe speed below.

High-End Modern Boards

e.g. X670E, Z790

T
Max Speed PCIe Gen 5.
S
Heatsink Mandatory for Gen 5 drives, usually included on the board.
P
Backwards Compat Yes, works perfectly with Gen 4 and Gen 3 drives.
C
Slot sharing Using Gen 5 M.2 might cut GPU PCIe lanes in half on some boards.

Standard Modern Boards

e.g. B650, B760

T
Max Speed PCIe Gen 4.
S
Heatsink Recommended for Gen 4 drives, often included on the board.
P
Backwards Compat Yes, works perfectly with Gen 3 drives.
C
Gen 5 Support Gen 5 drives will work, but will be limited to Gen 4 speeds.

Older / Budget Boards

e.g. B450, H610

T
Max Speed PCIe Gen 3.
S
Heatsink Optional. Gen 3 drives don't run very hot.
P
Gen 4 Support Gen 4 drives work, but are limited to Gen 3 speeds (3500 MB/s).
C
SATA ports Using an M.2 slot sometimes disables 2 SATA ports on older boards.
⚠️

Never use an HDD as your boot drive

Installing Windows on a mechanical hard drive (HDD) in 2026 is the single worst mistake you can make. It will make even a $3000 PC feel like a broken laptop from 2010. Boot times will take minutes instead of seconds, and the system will constantly stutter. Your primary C: drive MUST be an SSD (preferably NVMe).

🔧

Don't lose the tiny M.2 screw

NVMe M.2 drives don't use cables, but they do require a tiny standoff and screw to hold them flat against the motherboard. These screws are included in your MOTHERBOARD box, not the SSD box. Do not screw the M.2 drive down at an angle without the standoff beneath it, or you will snap the drive in half.

⚡
// SPACE-PC GOLDEN TIP

For gaming, Gen 4 is all you need.

Don't let marketing fool you. While Gen 5 SSDs boast 14,000 MB/s speeds, games do not load large enough single files to benefit from it. In real-world gaming tests, a top-tier Gen 5 drive loads games maybe 0.5 seconds faster than a solid Gen 4 drive, but costs twice as much and requires a massive cooler. Save your money and put it toward a better GPU instead.
