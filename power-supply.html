// COMPONENT 05

Power Supply.
The Heart.

It delivers life to every single component. A good one will run quietly for a decade. A bad one can take your entire PC to the grave.

In plain English: The Power Supply Unit (PSU) pulls electricity from your wall outlet and safely distributes it to your motherboard, CPU, GPU, and drives. It is the most critical part for system stability.
Jump to: Cable Types How Much? Standards Buying Guide Compatibility Golden Tip
// THE BIG CHOICE

Modular vs Non-Modular.

Power supplies come in different cable configurations. The choice you make here dictates how easy your PC will be to build and how clean it will look.

Fully Modular

// THE MODERN STANDARD · CLEAN BUILD

Every single cable can be detached from the power supply. You only plug in the cables you actually need for your components. This eliminates clutter, improves airflow, and makes building much easier.

Key specs

  • Cable clutter — Zero
  • Ease of build — Excellent
  • Custom cables — Supported
  • Price — Slightly more expensive

Non-Modular

// OLDER BUDGET STYLE · ALL ATTACHED

All cables are permanently attached to the PSU unit. Even if you don't need a specific cable, you still have to hide it somewhere in your case. Only recommended for strict budget builds.

Key specs

  • Cable clutter — High
  • Ease of build — Difficult
  • Custom cables — Not supported
  • Price — Cheaper
// THE REAL QUESTION

How much Wattage?

Your PSU must provide enough power (Watts) to feed your CPU and GPU at maximum load, plus a 20% safety buffer. Here is a simple breakdown based on modern parts.

🔋

500W – 650W

Entry-Level Builds

Perfect for budget systems with mid-range graphics cards (like RTX 4060 or RX 7600) and standard CPUs. Leaves enough headroom for safe operation.

Use case: 1080p Gaming, Office PCs

⚡

750W – 850W

The Sweet Spot

The standard recommendation for most modern gaming builds. Provides plenty of juice for powerful GPUs (like RTX 4070 Ti or RX 7800 XT) and allows for future upgrades.

Use case: 1440p/4K Gaming, Core i7/Ryzen 7

🚀

1000W+

Enthusiast / Pro

Required for top-tier monster GPUs (like the RTX 4090) paired with power-hungry flagship CPUs (like Core i9). Complete peace of mind for the heaviest workloads.

Use case: RTX 4090, Extreme Overclocking

// 2026 SNAPSHOT

Modern standards.

The stickers and certifications you'll see on PSU boxes — and what they actually mean. Efficiency, form factors, and new cables: here's your decoder.

Standard What it means Status (2026)
80 PLUS Gold The sweet spot for efficiency. Converts ~90% of wall power into PC power. Wastes very little as heat. MODERN
80 PLUS Bronze Entry-level efficiency (~85%). Good for budget builds, but runs slightly hotter than Gold. BUDGET
ATX 3.0 / PCIe 5.0 A new standard designed to handle massive power spikes from modern GPUs. Includes the new 12VHPWR cable. MODERN
12VHPWR Cable The new 16-pin cable required for RTX 4000 series GPUs. Delivers up to 600W through a single cord. MODERN
Form Factor: ATX The standard physical size for most power supplies. Fits in almost any mid-tower or full-tower case. MODERN
Form Factor: SFX A highly compacted power supply designed exclusively for tiny Mini-ITX PC cases. SFF ONLY
// PICK YOUR PSU

Buying guide.

Never buy a no-name power supply. Sticking to reputable brands (Corsair, Seasonic, EVGA) with at least an 80+ rating is the only rule you cannot break.

🏷️

Budget Build

~ $60 – $80

A 650W 80 PLUS Bronze (Non or Semi-Modular). It provides safe, reliable power for an entry-level GPU without breaking the bank.

Pick: 650W 80+ Bronze

⚡

Gaming Build

~ $110 – $140

An 850W 80 PLUS Gold (Fully Modular) with ATX 3.0 support. The absolute sweet spot. It runs quiet, keeps cables clean, and supports modern graphics cards natively.

Pick: 850W 80+ Gold (ATX 3.0)

🖥️

Creator / Pro

~ $200 – $300+

A 1000W+ 80 PLUS Gold or Platinum. When you are pushing an RTX 4090 and an i9 processor to their limits for 3D rendering, you need raw, stable wattage.

Pick: 1000W+ 80+ Gold/Platinum

⚠️

Never mix modular cables from different brands

If you are upgrading your modular PSU, DO NOT leave the old cables in your PC and plug them into the new unit. Even between models of the same brand, the pin layouts on the PSU side are often different. Mixing cables will instantly fry your motherboard, storage drives, and GPU when you press the power button. Always use the cables that came in the box with your new PSU.

💣

Don't cheap out on the PSU

It is the only component in your PC connected to 110V/220V wall power. A cheap, unrated, $30 power supply from an unknown brand lacks the critical safety protections (OVP, OCP, SCP). If there is a power surge, a good PSU will sacrifice itself to save your PC. A bad PSU will explode and take your $1000 GPU and CPU to the grave with it.

⚡
// SPACE-PC GOLDEN TIP

The 20% Headroom Rule.

Use a site like PCPartPicker to calculate the total estimated wattage of your build. Take that number, add 20-30%, and buy a power supply of that size. For example, if your system draws 500W, buy a 650W PSU. Power supplies are most efficient and quietest when running at 50-70% of their total capacity. Pushing a PSU to 100% capacity constantly will make the fan spin like a jet engine and shorten its lifespan.
